Quick Rule Summary:

  • A few: Used with countable nouns (things you can count). Means a small number.
  • A little: Used with uncountable nouns (things you can't count). Means a small amount.
  • Example: "I have a few apples." (apples are countable)
  • Example: "I need a little sugar." (sugar is uncountable)
  • Example: "She has a few friends." (friends are countable)
  • Example: "We have a little time." (time is uncountable)
  • Tip: Ask "Can I count it?" If yes, use "a few". If no, use "a little".
